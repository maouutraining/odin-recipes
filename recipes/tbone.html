TBone Steak

Description

T-bones, cut from the front section of the cow's short loin where the tenderloin narrows, command a premium price as popular special-occasion orders at upscale restaurants.

Most steakhouses cook beef over open flames, and the T-bone makes a good candidate for your backyard grill. Take care, though: The leaner tenderloin cooks more quickly than the strip side of this dual cut, so you need to keep it farther from the flame to achieve your desired doneness for the meat on both sides of the bone.

Ingredients

  • T-bone Steak
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Italian Seasoning
  • Compound Butter
  • Fresh Herbs

Steps

  1. Take Steak Out of Fridge, Preheat Oven
  2. Pat Dry, Add Seasonings
  3. Heat Skillet
  4. Sear
  5. Transfer to Oven
  6. Cook
  7. Rest Then Serve
